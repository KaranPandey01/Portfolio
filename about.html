About me

Systems thinker.
Backend builder.

I'm a Computer Science undergraduate at SRM Institute of Science and Technology, Delhi NCR, specializing in systems engineering, distributed infrastructure, and machine learning pipelines. I don't just write code — I build things that hold up under real load.

My R&D internship at IIT Kanpur (YRRNA System Labs) shaped how I think about production systems — working directly on distributed storage, cache consistency across 100+ nodes, and crash-recovery testing. I'm drawn to problems where correctness and performance both matter — distributed consensus, query optimization, real-time inference. If the system is interesting and the stakes are real, I'm in.

How I work

Performance-first
I measure before I optimize, and I always have the numbers to back up my changes. Every PR I ship has a concrete before/after metric.
Correctness under failure
Good systems fail gracefully. I write deterministic tests for crash recovery, data corruption, and edge cases — not just the happy path.
Clean, reusable code
I apply OOP principles and design patterns not because it looks good in a PR, but because it pays off in maintainability when systems scale.
Reproducible deploys
Docker-first workflow, env parity across local and prod. "Works on my machine" is not acceptable for systems I own.
Full-spectrum thinking
From distributed cache nodes to Next.js frontends and ML pipelines — I understand the full stack and design for the entire system, not just my layer.
R&D collaboration
At IIT Kanpur, I worked directly with engineers and researchers. I present findings, ask hard questions, and deliver production-ready code on deadline.
